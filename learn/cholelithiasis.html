Clinical scenario 1: Cholelithiasis (Gallstones)

Pathology: Formation of gallstones. There are 3 different types.

  • • Cholesterol: Excess cholesterol excretion by the liver, unable to be dissolved by bile, precipitates as crystals.
  • • Pigment (Bilirubin): Yellow biproduct of red blood cell breakdown, produced in excess by liver and secreted into bile.
  • • Mixed: Concentrated bile due to impaired gallbladder contractility

Risk factors: Family history, female sex, high fat/cholesterol in diet, overweight/obese, hormone replacement therapy, birth control, pregnancy
Symptoms: Poorly localized visceral (dull) abdominal pain. Sporadic and unpredictable. Worse on onset and relieved with time. Lasts 1-5h
Ultrasound: Gallstones seen (illustration)


Treatment: Monitoring if asymptomatic. Cholecystectomy is indicated when symptoms are severe and frequent enough to impact the patient’s quality of life
