Cholecystectomy

  1. Identify the liver and gallbladder
  2. Expose gallbladder by retraction
  3. Identify the B-safe landmarks and note any anatomical variation. Orientate self at each “time-out” in surgery. Abort procedure if gallbladder cannot be visualised.
    1. B = bile duct, Base segment 4
    2. S = Sulcus Rouviere's, segment 4
    3. A = Artery Hepatic
    4. F = FIssure umblical
    5. E = Enteric structure
  4. View hepato-cystic triangle (lower border of liver, cystic duct, common hepatic duct)
  5. clear any fatty tissue away from triangle
  6. achieve Critical View of Safety (CVS)
  7. identify cystic duct + cystic artery within clear hepato-cystic triangle
  8. partial separation of gallbladder from liver = expose only lower 1/3 of cystic plate (where gallbladder attaches to liver)
  9. if too much cystic plate exposed = risk of gallbladder twisting
  10. 2 (and ONLY 2) structures should be seen entering gallbladder
  11. apply clip to cystic artery + cystic duct, at neck of gallbladder
  12. clips = 2 proximal clips + 1 distal clip
  13. divide cystic artery + cystic duct (cut between 2nd proximal clip + distal clip)
  14. separate gallbladder from liver bed (aspirate any blood/bile that accumulated)
  15. extract gallbladder
  16. closure
