Clinical Scenario 2: Acute Cholecystitis

Pathology: Blocked outflow of bile at cystic duct resulting in inflammation of the gallbladder. Usually secondary to cholelithiasis. Obstruction of cystic duct can result in secondary infections: E. Coli, Enterococcus, Klebsiella, Enterobacter.
Risk factors: Same as cholelithiasis
Symptoms: Abdominal pain located to the right upper quadrant (epigastric area), may radiate to right shoulder. Prologued, continuous, and worsening pain. Very painful 15-20 minutes after eating. Fever from secondary infection.
Ultrasound: Cystic duct blockage, gallbladder wall thickening >3mm (Illustration)


Treatment: Admission to hospital: fasting, IV fluids, painkillers, antibiotics if infection is suspected.
Gallbladder removal is recommended to prevent further complications after the initial inflammation has settled down.
